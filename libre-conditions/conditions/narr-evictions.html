The Eviction-Violation Connection

Evictions and housing violations are two sides of the same coin. Tenants who report violations often face retaliatory eviction filings — even though retaliation is illegal. Meanwhile, tenants living in unsafe conditions may fall behind on rent due to related expenses (medical costs from mold, replacing broken appliances, temporary housing during repairs), putting them at risk of eviction for nonpayment.

The heat map above shows how eviction filings cluster in specific neighborhoods. When you compare this to the violations dashboard, the overlap is striking: the neighborhoods with the most code violations are the same ones with the most evictions.

The cycle: Disinvestment → deteriorating conditions → violations → landlord retaliation or rent increases → eviction → displacement. This cycle repeats block by block, building by building, across the same communities that were redlined a century ago.
