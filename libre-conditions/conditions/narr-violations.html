What the Violations Data Shows

Renters and their families deserve to live in safe, healthy homes. Housing violations jeopardize that right — they represent conditions that affect health and safety as determined by law. As you explore the dashboard above, the systemic patterns become clear.

Many of the same areas with high numbers of code violations coincide with historically disinvested parts of Long Beach that were once redlined or contained racial covenants. The buildings in these neighborhoods are older, have received less investment, and are more likely to be owned by entities focused on extracting rent rather than maintaining quality housing.

For renters: If you are living with code violations — mold, pests, broken plumbing, or other dangerous conditions — you have the right to report them. Retaliation by landlords for reporting violations is illegal under California law.
