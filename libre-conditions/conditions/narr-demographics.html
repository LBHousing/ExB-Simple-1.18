Housing Justice is Racial Justice

The data makes the connection undeniable. Long Beach's Black and African American community has faced a housing struggle rooted in decades of discriminatory policy. Latino families face some of the highest rent burden rates in the city. Southeast Asian communities — particularly Cambodian refugees who rebuilt their lives in Long Beach — are concentrated in areas with high violation counts and aging housing stock.

These patterns are not accidents of the market. They are the direct, traceable legacy of policies that determined where people of color could live, whether they could own property, and how much investment their neighborhoods would receive. Understanding this history is essential to building policies that repair the damage rather than perpetuate it.

The data tools on this page exist for a reason: to make the invisible visible. When you can see the violations, map the evictions, and overlay the demographics, the pattern becomes impossible to ignore — and impossible to dismiss.
